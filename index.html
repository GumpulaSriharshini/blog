Introduction

The rise of social media platforms has transformed how we communicate and interact online. While these platforms offer unprecedented connectivity, they have also given rise to cyberbullying, a serious problem affecting users worldwide, particularly young people.

Cyberbullying represents one of the most significant challenges to digital well-being in the modern era, with far-reaching psychological and social consequences for victims.

This project addresses this critical issue by developing an advanced AI-powered system capable of detecting and mitigating cyberbullying effectively. By combining cutting-edge machine learning with natural language processing techniques, we aim to create safer online environments for all users.

Our research focuses on YouTube comments as a primary data source, where the anonymity and reach of the platform often contribute to higher instances of online harassment.

Our comprehensive approach to cyberbullying detection combines data collection, preprocessing, feature engineering, and model development:

Model Training

Multiple models were developed and compared, including traditional ML approaches (Logistic Regression, SVM, Random Forest) and deep learning architectures (LSTM, RNN).

Data Annotation

Manual labeling of comments as bullying or non-bullying by trained annotators with inter-rater reliability checks.

Model Selection

Comparative analysis of model performance using cross-validation on balanced datasets.

Hyperparameter Tuning

Grid search optimization to find ideal parameters for each model architecture.

Deployment

Integration with Streamlit for user-friendly interface and real-time detection capabilities.

Results

Our comprehensive evaluation demonstrated that deep learning models, particularly LSTM networks, significantly outperformed traditional machine learning approaches for cyberbullying detection.

Model Performance

LSTM: 81.56%
SVM: 76.20%
Random Forest: 73.40%

The LSTM model's superior performance can be attributed to its ability to capture sequential patterns and contextual information in text data, which is crucial for understanding the nuanced nature of cyberbullying.

In addition to overall accuracy, our LSTM model demonstrated excellent precision (79.2%) and recall (80.7%), indicating a balanced ability to identify bullying content while minimizing false positives.

The deployed Streamlit application enables real-time detection with response times under 1.5 seconds, making it suitable for integration with high-traffic platforms.

Conclusion

Our AI-powered cyberbullying detection system represents a significant step forward in creating safer online environments. By leveraging advanced deep learning architectures, we have demonstrated that automated systems can effectively identify harmful content with high accuracy.

The combination of AI and human moderation presents the most promising approach to addressing cyberbullying at scale while respecting the nuances of human communication.

Future Directions

While our current implementation shows promising results, several avenues for enhancement exist:

  • Cross-platform integration: Expanding detection capabilities to multiple social media platforms
  • Multilingual support: Developing models capable of detecting cyberbullying in various languages
  • Advanced NLP techniques: Implementing transformer-based models like BERT for improved contextual understanding
  • Intervention strategies: Developing systems that not only detect but also suggest appropriate interventions

This project demonstrates the potential of AI as a powerful tool in the ongoing effort to combat online harassment and create digital spaces where users can engage safely and constructively.
